1 Pioneer account & KYC prerequisites

Before you can register an app at all, the Pi account submitting it has to be a real, verified Pioneer — not a fresh or dormant account.

  • •
    Active Pioneer account in good standing on the Pi Network mobile app, with a history of mining/activity (brand-new accounts are more likely to be scrutinized).
  • •
    KYC (Know-Your-Customer) verification completed — this is required to submit any app for Mainnet listing, not optional. Do this from the Pi mobile app's KYC section; it can take from a few days to several weeks depending on the backlog.
  • •
    A Pi Wallet set up and unlocked (needed if the app will ever receive Pi payments, and needed to sign the Developer Portal's terms).
  • •
    Access to the Pi Browser app (Developer Portal and Testnet apps only run inside Pi Browser, not a normal desktop/mobile browser).
These steps happen entirely inside the Pi mobile app / Pi Browser under your personal identity — no code or file we build for you can complete KYC or account verification on your behalf.

2 Developer Portal registration & app setup

Once your Pioneer account is KYC-verified, you register the app itself.

  1. Open the Pi Browser app on your phone and navigate to the Develop tab (this is the Pi Developer Portal — it only renders inside Pi Browser).
  2. Choose Create App and give it the name DukuDukuChat, a short description, and a category.
  3. Fill in the app's Development URL (a Testnet/staging deployment of DukuDukuChat's Pi-Browser build) and later the Production URL (your live Mainnet domain).
  4. Domain verification: the Portal will ask you to prove you control the domain — typically by hosting a small verification file (or DNS TXT record) at a path it gives you. Your deployed backend/static host must serve that file exactly as instructed.
  5. Configure the API key / app credentials the Portal issues — your server's Pi token-verification calls (already implemented in server/src/auth/verifyPi.js) use these against https://api.minepi.com/v2/me.
  6. Fill in the app icon, screenshots, short/long descriptions, and support contact fields in the Portal listing form.
  7. Agree to the Pi Platform / App Studio terms presented in the Portal.

3 Testnet → Mainnet workflow

Pi apps are never submitted straight to Mainnet. The Developer Portal enforces a staged rollout.

StageWhat happensCurrency
Development / TestnetApp runs from the Development URL, only visible to you and testers you add. All Pi payments use worthless Test-Pi so you can fully exercise the wallet/tipping flow without risk.Test-Pi (no value)
Review / Hackathon or Mainnet submissionYou submit the app through the Portal for Pi Network Team review against the Mainnet Listing Requirements (section 4 below). Reviewers test the live Development URL.—
MainnetOnce approved, the Production URL goes live in the official Pi App Directory inside Pi Browser, and transactions use real Pi.Real Pi
Practical implication for DukuDukuChat: deploy the DukuDukuChat_Web.html front end + the Node/Express server/ backend to a real host (Render, Railway, your own VPS, etc.) first, point the Portal's Development URL at that deployment, and only request Mainnet review once you've tested login, posting, chat and tipping end-to-end with Test-Pi.

Keep the Google+Apple+Pi version (as built in DukuDukuChat_Web.html) as your separate "standalone" build for outside Pi Browser, and ship a trimmed, Pi-only build as the one you actually submit to the Directory. 5 Content & community guidelines (Pi App Studio)

Separate from the technical Mainnet rules, Pi App Studio's Community Guidelines govern what the app can actually contain and how it can behave, since DukuDukuChat is a social/UGC app.

  • •
    Accurate intent — the app must do what it claims to do in its listing; no bait-and-switch functionality.
  • •
    No manipulation of Pi as a token — no schemes to artificially inflate perceived Pi value, no pyramid/referral-for-Pi schemes, no promises of guaranteed returns.
  • •
    No fraud, scams, or defamatory content, and (being a social network) DukuDukuChat needs real moderation tooling for user-generated posts, comments, chat, and video — reporting/blocking, not just an honor system.
  • •
    Protection of personal data — align with requirement 7 above; publish what you collect and why.
  • •
    No facilitation of illegal conduct through chat, posts, or the marketplace-like "mini-apps" (Split Bill, Polls, QR Connect) — keep these clearly framed as social utilities.
  • •
    Pi Network Team can suspend or delist apps that violate these guidelines post-launch, so ongoing moderation matters, not just a one-time review.
Because DukuDukuChat combines Facebook/WeChat/TikTok/Telegram/Instagram-style features (feed, stories, video, chat, tipping) it is a higher-scrutiny category than a simple utility app. Budget real time for a report/block/moderation-queue feature before Mainnet submission — this is not yet built in server/ and should be treated as a launch-blocking gap, not a nice-to-have.

Brand-colour self-check for DukuDukuChat: the existing brand system is built on violet/indigo (#6C4FF5 / #4B32C3) and coral (#FF5C7A), with a small gold accent dot (#FFD24C) used only as a tiny highlight on the logo mark. This palette is visually distinct from Pi Network's own black/white/gold branding as a whole, so it's unlikely to read as impersonation — but because the gold accent dot could visually echo Pi's colour associations, it's worth a quick manual comparison against the current official Pi Network brand assets right before you submit, and swapping the accent dot to the coral or violet tones if reviewers flag it.

9 What only you can do next
